I grew up believing I shared an unbreakable bond with a gerbil named Gerald. He, however, remained blissfully unaware of this connection, leading me to an early fascination with tragicomic narratives.

When not attempting to appear profoundly literary (a process involving turtlenecks, excessive sighing, and the occasional brooding gaze into the distance), I can be found crafting stories that straddle the absurd, the eerie, and the outright ridiculous. My work, a mix of dark whimsy and unsettling hilarity, invites readers to embrace the peculiar and question reality—often while laughing nervously.

Currently residing on a rock, I continue to write, occasionally pausing to wonder whether Gerald is out there somewhere, composing gerbil sonnets under the floorboards.
